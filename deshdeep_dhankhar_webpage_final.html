Deshdeep Dhankhar

Researcher on Political Behaviour, Democracy, and Opposition

About

Deshdeep Dhankhar is currently engaged in a project on local democracy and innovations at the grassroots at the Max Weber Forum for South Asian Studies (MWF) and the Centre de Sciences Humaines (CSH), New Delhi. His research focuses on political behaviour, democracy, and opposition, with a keen interest in exploring various facets of democratic processes using contemporary methodological tools.

Before his current engagement at the MWF-CSH, he served as a Policy Fellow at the Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS), Mumbai. Deshdeep has worked with academics and organizations such as CSDS-Lokniti and OXFAM India. He holds a Master’s in Political Science from the University of Hyderabad and a Bachelor’s in Political Science from the University of Delhi.
